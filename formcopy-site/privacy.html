What FormCopy Pro Does

FormCopy Pro is a Google Sheets add-on that automatically copies formulas from row 2 into every new form response row. It helps users maintain calculated fields, lookups, and other formulas across all response rows without manual effort.

Data We Access

FormCopy Pro requests the following permissions to function:

  • Spreadsheet data (spreadsheets) — We read formulas and number formats from row 2 of the linked response sheet and write those formulas into new response rows. We do not access any other spreadsheets in your Google Drive.
  • External requests (script.external_request) — Used for license validation only.
  • User email (userinfo.email) — We access your email address to identify your account for license and usage tracking.
  • Container UI (script.container.ui) — Used to display the add-on sidebar and configuration dialogs within Google Sheets.

Data We Collect

None. All data stays in your Google Sheets. FormCopy Pro does not collect, store, or transmit any spreadsheet data, form responses, or personal information to external servers.

Data Storage

All user settings (such as feature preferences and usage counters) are stored exclusively in Google Apps Script's Properties Service. This data resides within Google's infrastructure, tied to your Google account.

We do not operate any external servers or databases. No spreadsheet data or user information is ever transmitted to or stored on third-party servers.

Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or disclose any user data to third parties. Your spreadsheet data and personal information remain within the Google ecosystem at all times.

Data Retention

Settings are retained in Google Properties Service for as long as the add-on is installed. Uninstalling the add-on removes all stored settings. We do not retain any data after uninstallation.

Security

FormCopy Pro runs entirely within Google's Apps Script platform, inheriting Google's security infrastructure. We do not handle authentication or store credentials independently.
