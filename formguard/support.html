Quick start

Setup in 3 steps

  1. Open Google Forms™ in edit mode and launch FormGuard from Extensions.
  2. Choose one limit type: total responses, choice quota, close message, or owner notification.
  3. Run a test submission and check the public form before sharing it.

Reviewer notes

  • Google Forms™ access is used to read and update the active form state, limits, and choice availability.
  • Mail access is used only when owner notifications are enabled.
  • The add-on does not require a separate username, password, or API token beyond Google account access.

FAQ

Common questions

Does it replace Google Forms™?

No. It adds guardrails on top of your existing Google Forms™ workflow.

What should I test first?

Use a copy of the form, submit once, and confirm the close or limit behavior.

Where do I get help?

Use the FormGuard support form or email link above, and include the form use case plus what limit failed.

What should I avoid promising?

FormGuard is a practical control layer for Google Forms™. It is not a strict reservation backend, an automatic waitlist promotion system, or a quantity-aware inventory engine.
