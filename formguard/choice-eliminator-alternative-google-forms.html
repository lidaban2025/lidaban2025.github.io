Short answer

Choice Eliminator is useful when your main job is removing or limiting choices after they reach capacity. FormGuard is built for teams who need to control what happens after people start submitting: response caps, choice quotas, alerts, and launch rules.

This page is intentionally precise: FormGuard is not claiming to copy every Choice Eliminator feature. It is an alternative when your real problem is Google Forms limits and operational control.

FAQ

Is FormGuard a Choice Eliminator replacement?

It is an alternative when your goal is Google Forms choice limits plus response limits, notifications, and timers. It is not positioned as a clone of every Choice Eliminator behavior.

Can FormGuard limit each choice in Google Forms?

Yes. FormGuard supports choice quota workflows for limited options like slots, sessions, and classes.

Can I also limit total form responses?

Yes. You can use response limits when the whole form has an overall cap.
