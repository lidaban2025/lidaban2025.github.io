Quick answer

Most forms need one of these three controls. Pick the smallest one that solves the workflow.

Only need one response per person?

Use native Google Forms settings first. Google documents the built-in "Limit to 1 response" option in its form sharing guide.

Need the form to stop at a cap or date?

Use the native close date or response limit first. Google Forms now supports both from the published form response settings.

Need individual choices to disappear?

Use FormGuard choice quotas when workshops, time slots, volunteer roles, or limited options fill separately.

Setup map

  • Open the form in edit mode and confirm whether the limit is by total responses, close date, or choice availability.
  • Use Google Forms' native close date or response limit for simple form-level stopping.
  • Install FormGuard from Google Workspace Marketplace when you need choice quotas, owner alerts, or a guided add-on workflow.
  • Save one rule first, test it on a disposable form, then add notifications or a closed message.
  • After the first successful setup, use the support page for troubleshooting and permissions context.
