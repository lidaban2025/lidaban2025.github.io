Quick answer

Most forms need one of these three controls. Pick the smallest one that solves the workflow.

Only need one response per person?

Use native Google Forms settings first. Google documents the built-in "Limit to 1 response" option in its form sharing guide.

Need the form to stop at a cap or date?

Use FormGuard for response limits, scheduled close windows, closed-form messages, and owner alerts.

Need individual choices to disappear?

Use a choice quota when workshops, time slots, volunteer roles, or limited options fill separately.

Setup map

  • Open the form in edit mode and confirm whether the limit is by total responses, deadline, or choice availability.
  • Install FormGuard from Google Workspace Marketplace if native settings are not enough.
  • Save one rule first, test it on a disposable form, then add owner notifications or a closed message.
  • After the first successful setup, use the support page for troubleshooting and permissions context.
