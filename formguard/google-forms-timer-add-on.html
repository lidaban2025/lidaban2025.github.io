The problem

Native Google Forms can make quizzes, but timed tests and controlled launches are more fragile. Teachers, trainers, and teams often need a safer workflow: test first, open at the right time, close clearly, and avoid collecting late responses.

FormGuard is designed for Google Forms users who want practical controls without rebuilding the form in a heavier form builder.

How to do it with FormGuard

  1. Install FormGuard: Add the FormGuard Google Forms add-on.
  2. Create or open the quiz form: Use a test copy before the real quiz if stakes are high.
  3. Configure timer-style workflow: Set the timing, availability, or launch control behavior that matches your quiz.
  4. Add close instructions: Explain what happens after the timer or availability window ends.
  5. Run a rehearsal: Submit as a student or participant and check the experience.

FAQ

Does Google Forms have a built-in timer?

Google Forms does not provide a full native timer experience for every quiz workflow. Many users use an add-on for timer-style controls.

Can FormGuard help with timed quizzes?

Yes. FormGuard supports timer-style and launch-control workflows alongside response limits and notifications.

Can I test before giving the quiz to students?

Yes. Testing on a copy or disposable form is recommended before sharing the real quiz.

Should I use this for high-stakes exams?

For high-stakes exams, use dedicated exam software. FormGuard is better for lightweight Google Forms quiz and training workflows.
