Policy

The commercial model stays intentionally quiet in phase 1 so support, first-run success, and Marketplace trust improve before packaging logic becomes a public promise.

Public path

No public checkout as the primary CTA during launch mode No public plan table on the main site Keep the site focused on install, test, support, and feedback

License model

Do not describe license enforcement as live Keep add-ons installable from Marketplace while launch-mode testing continues Use first-run feedback to decide which limits need clearer packaging later

Support promise

Single support mailbox for all tools Product-specific support pages stay on the same domain Review requests happen after install and a meaningful successful action

FAQ

Why is installation the first step?

Right now the site needs installs, first-run success, feedback, and Marketplace trust. Visitors should choose an add-on, install it, and test a small workflow.

What should someone do first?

The clearest public path today is to install one of the five published add-ons from Google Workspace Marketplace and test a small workflow.

Which add-ons are safe to recommend right now?

As of May 8, 2026, recommend DocForge, FormGuard, FormNotifier, FormCopy, and FormMerge as installable Marketplace products, use FormFlow as a pilot page, and keep FormRanger positioned through fit, support, and use-case clarity while review work continues.

What happens during Google review?

Most add-ons stay in free launch mode while review is active. DocForge, FormGuard, FormNotifier, FormCopy, and FormMerge are now published and available on the Marketplace. FormFlow and FormRanger should stay in pilot or fit-first positioning until their public install path is ready.
